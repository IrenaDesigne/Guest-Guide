Cultural Landmarks

  • Diocletian’s Palace – A 1700-year-old Roman palace and the historical heart of Split. Highlights: Peristyle Square, the Cathedral of St. Domnius, Roman substructures, and an ancient Egyptian sphinx. $1
  • Marjan Hill – A peaceful forest park with panoramic viewpoints, old chapels, and scenic walking trails. Ideal for a refreshing walk, panoramic photos, or peaceful sunset moments.
  • Meštrović Gallery – Elegant seaside villa showcasing sculptures by Croatia’s greatest artist, Ivan Meštrović. A cultural gem offering a tranquil artistic experience with a sea view.
  • Prokurative Square – Venetian-style plaza lined with charming cafés, often hosting live music and cultural events. A perfect blend of architecture, culture, and café culture in the city center.
  • City & Archaeological Museums – Discover Split’s rich past through curated exhibitions and rare historical artifacts. Uncover layers of history from prehistoric times to Roman and medieval Split.
  • Church of St. Francis – A tranquil coastal church with stunning interiors and a quiet spiritual atmosphere. Step inside for peace, beauty, and a touch of medieval elegance.
